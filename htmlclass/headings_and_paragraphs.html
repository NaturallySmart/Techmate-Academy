HTML Headings

Headings are very important in HTML and website design. Search engines use the headings to index the structure and content of your web pages.

Users often skim a page by its headings. It is important to use headings to show the document structure.

Headings are defined by the h1 to h6 tags. 

            <h1>Heading Level One</h1>
            <h2>Heading Level Two</h2>
            ...


The most important headings are defined by the h1 tag down to the h6 tag.






HTML Paragraphs

In HTML, paragraphs are created using the <p> tag. A paragraph typically contains a few sentences or a short piece of text that makes sense on its own. It can be used as a follow-up content to the heading or to explain it just as it is in your textbooks.

The <hr> tag defines a thematic break in an HTML page, and is most often displayed as a horizontal rule. It is an empty tag as it has no closing tag

The <br> tag defines a line break. You can use it to start a new line without starting a new paragraph.
